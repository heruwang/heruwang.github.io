Fictional website: Flower Delivery

Tool: Illustrator, Photoshop, Sketch

Role: Visual design, User Interface

Design Concept

This flower website is targeting residents in the city who have no leisure time to shop at flower markets and do not want to be stuck in the traffic. I use pastel hues to give the city-based users a relaxing visual experience. Therefore, this design has to be easy to use, relaxing to look at, and feel clean and simple.

Typography

I mainly used font Lucida Grande for the majority of the font with some Baskerville when it comes to names. I love Lucida Grande since it has the simplest style. Baskerville is used to create a contrast. For example, I use Baskerville for the description of the website on the landing page because it sets a difference between contents. I use Baskerville for customer’s name and address since it distincts customers’ information from other information.

Overall Design

  1. Flowers is typed in a different font to emphasize the theme of the website.
  2. Illustration works on the landing page has been a trend. I used a city background in illustration since the targeted customers live in the city.
  3. The simple sketch of flowers insist the style of simplicity and nature. It reminds the customers what they are signed up for (a flower website).
  4. Many times when customers open their order history is when they want to order the same product again. Therefore, I put an order again button right next to the purchased item to bring users some convenience.
  5. I believe quantity number is important to customers. There must be a quantity next to each purchasing items in order to help them check if they are making the right purchase.
